1. Introduction

1.1 Background

Urban expansion is a critical concern for sustainable city planning. Understanding where and how cities grow enables planners to anticipate infrastructure needs, protect environmentally sensitive areas, optimize land use allocation, and estimate environmental impacts.

Münster, a mid-sized German city with approximately 320,000 inhabitants, serves as an ideal case study due to its well-documented OpenStreetMap coverage and characteristic suburban growth patterns.

1.2 Objectives

  1. Detect semiannual urban growth patterns using OSM building data
  2. Analyze spatial clustering and directional trends
  3. Forecast future development areas
  4. Estimate environmental impacts (tree canopy loss, CO2)
  5. Visualize findings through interactive maps and dashboards

1.3 Study Area

  • City: Münster, North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany
  • Bounding Box: 7.47°E - 7.77°E, 51.84°N - 52.06°N
  • Area: ~302 km²
  • Coordinate Systems: WGS84 (EPSG:4326) for acquisition, UTM 32N (EPSG:25832) for analysis

2.2 Spatial Analysis Methods

Global Moran's I

Moran's I statistic measures the degree of spatial clustering:

I = (n / Σᵢ Σⱼ wᵢⱼ) × (Σᵢ Σⱼ wᵢⱼ(xᵢ - x̄)(xⱼ - x̄)) / (Σᵢ (xᵢ - x̄)²)

Where n = number of spatial units, wᵢⱼ = spatial weight, xᵢ = attribute value.

LISA Clusters

Local Indicators of Spatial Association identify local clusters and outliers:

  • High-High (HH): Hot spots - high values surrounded by high values
  • Low-Low (LL): Cold spots - low values surrounded by low values
  • High-Low (HL): Spatial outliers
  • Low-High (LH): Spatial outliers

2.3 Forecasting Methods

An ensemble approach combined three methods:

  1. Linear Trend Extrapolation
  2. Exponential Smoothing (Holt's Method)
  3. Moving Average

Development potential scoring weighted: Recent growth (35%), Neighbor growth (30%), Trend (20%), Distance (15%).

2.4 Environmental Impact Estimation

Canopy_Loss = Buildings × Footprint × Dev_Multiplier × Canopy_Coverage(zone)

Where: Footprint = 150 m², Dev_Multiplier = 1.8×, Canopy_Coverage = 15-55% by zone.

4. Discussion

4.1 Interpretation

The southward suburban expansion aligns with transportation corridor development (A1 highway) and available developable land. The "suburban ring effect" (peak growth at 6-10 km) reflects land availability constraints in the urban core and affordability considerations.

4.2 Limitations

  • OSM data quality varies spatially
  • Canopy estimates are model-based, not remotely sensed
  • Economic factors not incorporated in forecasts

4.3 Recommendations

  • Focus green infrastructure in the southern growth corridor
  • Implement tree preservation ordinances in the 6-10 km zone
  • Establish canopy targets for new developments (e.g., 20% minimum)
